The **AI for Good Global Summit** and **WSIS Forum 2026**, two flagship events of Geneva Digital Week hosted by the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), recently convened in Geneva, Switzerland. The twin UN summits brought together heads of state, international organisation leaders, academics, and industry figures to address artificial intelligence for good, global digital governance, and the implementation of Sustainable Development Goals. Xuebing Bai, Secretary-General of the Global Youth Governance Association (GYGA), was invited to attend throughout, engaging in multilateral exchanges, high-level dialogues, and international partnership discussions — demonstrating the commitment and agency of global youth in shaping global governance.

![GYGA Secretary-General Xuebing Bai at the AI for Good Global Summit 2026](/images/news/ai-for-good-2026-bai.jpg)

## High-Level Dialogue with Senior UN Officials

On 6 July, at the invitation of the Executive Director of the Global Centre for SDGs and Leadership Development, Xuebing Bai attended a thematic conference and awards ceremony co-organised by the Centre and the ITU. During the event, he held warm exchanges with **former UN Deputy Secretary-General Fabrizio** and **former WIPO Director-General Gao Rui**. The three parties engaged in in-depth discussions on SDG implementation, international talent development, global public governance, and digital cooperation — sharing cutting-edge perspectives and practical experience. This high-level face-to-face exchange strengthened GYGA's ties with senior UN networks and laid solid foundations for future collaboration.

![Xuebing Bai with former UN Deputy Secretary-General Fabrizio and former WIPO Director-General Gao Rui](/images/news/ai-for-good-2026-fabrizio.jpg)

## Entering the Palais des Nations: Strategic Partnership on Youth

On 7 July, at the invitation of **Academician Li Yutong, Executive President of the World Academy of Digital Technology Sciences and Innovation (WADTSI)**, Xuebing Bai attended a series of high-level thematic sessions at the United Nations Palais des Nations. During the event, he held a dedicated roundtable with Academician Li Yutong and the **Ambassador of Gambia to the United Nations**, focusing on the strategic partnership between WADTSI and GYGA, and reaching consensus on youth empowerment in the age of artificial intelligence.

![Xuebing Bai with Academician Li Yutong and the Gambian UN Ambassador · UN Palais des Nations](/images/news/ai-for-good-2026-meeting.jpg)

All three parties agreed that as digital technology and AI advance rapidly and the global governance landscape accelerates its transformation, youth represent the central driving force for sustainable development. Leveraging their respective platforms, WADTSI and GYGA will jointly build an international and professional youth development ecosystem — focusing on AI literacy, global governance capacity, and international perspective — to cultivate a new generation of young talent equipped with global competitiveness and a sense of responsibility, enabling more Chinese and global youth to participate deeply in international affairs and global governance.

## Partnership with UNRISD: Deepening Youth Development Cooperation

On 10 July, in the final days of the summit, Xuebing Bai held a dedicated exchange with officials from the **UN Research Institute for Social Development (UNRISD)**, focusing on youth empowerment, youth participation in global social development, and digital inclusion for youth. The two sides explored long-term cooperation mechanisms, exchanging ideas on leveraging UN platform resources to lower barriers for youth participation in international governance, establishing regular youth exchange channels, and mainstreaming youth issues into the global sustainable development agenda — setting clear directions for future collaboration in youth public service, international study programmes, governance practice, and talent development.

---

This series of engagements at UN summits in Geneva marks a significant milestone in GYGA's deepening integration with the UN system and its commitment to global youth governance. Going forward, GYGA will continue to advance youth empowerment with an international perspective, deepening multilateral partnerships with UN agencies, international academic platforms, and diplomatic missions — building high-level international exchange platforms to help more young people engage with the world and make their mark in the digital age and the evolving landscape of global governance.
